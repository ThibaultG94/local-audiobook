## 4) Challenges and Friction Points

### Core Friction (confirmed by Project Lead)
> Some robustness requirements were detected too late during review.

### Pattern Observed
- Teams corrected issues effectively, but multiple structural improvements landed late in the cycle.
- This increased rework pressure near completion and delayed confidence sign-off.

### Systemic Root Cause
- Pre-review quality gates were not strict enough to detect robustness issues before adversarial review.

---

## 5) Key Insights and Lessons Learned

1. **Foundational discipline compounds positively**
   - Early investment in contracts, migrations, and boundaries accelerated later stories.
2. **Late review catch is still expensive even when fixes are high quality**
   - The process should shift detection earlier.
3. **Definition of Done must include explicit robustness checks**
   - “Feature complete” is insufficient without resilience and boundary verification.
4. **Adversarial review is valuable, but should validate—not discover basics**
   - Baseline robustness should be pre-verified by the implementation workflow.
