1. **Contract consistency held across all extraction paths**
   - `{ok, data, error}` and normalized error payloads remained stable as scope expanded from EPUB to PDF and TXT/MD.
2. **Observability quality improved story-by-story**
   - Extraction lifecycle events and diagnostics presentation events became increasingly consistent and useful.
3. **Regression discipline stayed strong under iterative hardening**
   - Story records show repeated full-suite validation after adversarial review fixes.
4. **Actionable presenter feedback matured significantly**
   - User-facing extraction feedback became clearer and more deterministic, including retry semantics tied to `retryable`.

### Team Collaboration Highlights

- Review findings were absorbed quickly and converted into concrete quality improvements.
- Reuse of shared patterns (normalization utilities, contract anchors, centralized logging) reduced implementation drift.

## 4) Challenges and Friction Points

1. **Late robustness discoveries still occurred during code review**
   - Multiple stories required post-review hardening (imports, edge-case validation, diagnostics payload shaping).
2. **Cross-file consistency required repeated correction**
   - Absolute import paths, schema completeness, and error-detail typing needed iterative alignment.
3. **Diagnostics precision had to be tightened over time**
   - Event payload completeness and remediation wording required several passes to reach a reliable baseline.

### Systemic Root Cause

Pre-review quality gates improved vs Epic 1 but remained insufficiently strict to prevent recurring medium/high hardening fixes in late phases.

## 5) Deep Story Analysis — Patterns Across Epic 2

### Common Struggles

### Recurring Review Feedback

- Keep payload schema explicit and complete (`source_format`, correlation context, retry semantics).

- Unified diagnostics in Story 2.5 reduced cross-format support ambiguity.

### Testing and Quality Insights
